Rahul Dravid

"The Wall of Indian Cricket"

Here's the timeline of Rahul Dravid's life.

  • 1973 - Born in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India.
  • 1989 - Begins playing cricket at the age of 16 in local club matches in Bangalore.
  • 1991 - Represents Karnataka at the under-19 level, showcasing his immense talent early on.
  • 1996 - Makes his Test debut for India against England at Lord's, scoring a resilient 95 runs.
  • 1996 - Shortly thereafter, makes his One Day International (ODI) debut against Sri Lanka.
  • 2000 - Appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team, leading with poise and dedication.
  • 2002 - Scores a monumental 233 against Australia in Adelaide, instrumental in India's historic Test win.
  • 2004 - Achieves the accolade of scoring over 10,000 runs in both Test and ODI cricket formats.
  • 2005 - Wins the ICC Player of the Year and the Test Player of the Year awards.
  • 2007 - Guides India to a series victory in England, his leadership pivotal in the team's success.
  • 2011 - Scores three centuries in England, despite India's overall series loss, demonstrating his class and tenacity.
  • 2012 - Announces his retirement from international cricket, finishing with over 24,000 international runs.
  • 2014 - Appointed as the coach of the India A and Under-19 teams, nurturing the next generation of cricketers.
  • 2018 - Inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame, a testament to his outstanding contributions to the sport.
  • 2020 - Takes on the role of Head of Cricket at the National Cricket Academy in Bangalore, continuing to influence the sport's development in India.
